ADV:
adverb

Adverbs are words that typically modify [adjectives](ADJ), [verbs](VERB) or other adverbs for such categories as time, place, direction or manner. DET:
determiner

Determiners are words that modify [nouns](NOUN) or noun phrases and express the reference of the noun phrase in context. This is a closed class in Kazakh. Many determiners are ambiguous with [pronouns](PRON). #### Examples * [kk] _”ô—Ä –∞–¥–∞–º_ "every person" * [kk] _–±—ñ—Ä –∞–¥–∞–º_ "a person" (ambiguous with "one person") * [kk] _–∫–µ–π –∞–¥–∞–º_ "some people" * [kk] _“õ–∞–π –∞–¥–∞–º?_ "which person?"

NOUN:
noun

Nouns inflect for [case](Case), [number](Number) and [possession](Possession). Nouns receive nominal morphology. Other parts of speech may be derived into nouns, such as adjectives. Proper nouns are not annotated as `NOUN` but rather [PROPN](). #### Examples * [kk] _“õ—ã–∑_ "girl" * [kk] _“Ø–π_ "house" * [kk] _–∞“ì–∞—à_ "tree"

VERB:

Verbs typically inflect for [tense](Tense), [mood](Mood) and [person](Person) and signal events and actions. Verbs can constitute a minimal predicate in a clause, and govern the number and types of other constituents which may occur in the clause. Auxiliary and modal verbs are not annotated as `VERB` but rather [AUX](). #### Examples * [fi] _–æ–π–Ω–∞, –∂–µ_ "play, eat"
